Overview

The Product:

The project’s objective was to create a secure sign-in page specifically for administrators. This page would allow authorized users to access the system with appropriate credentials, ensuring that only authorized personnel can perform administrative tasks. The focus was on robust authentication mechanisms and user-friendly design to enhance security and usability.

The Goal:

Simplify Your Customer Communication with HOOTS. Communicates with the customers by integrating with popular messaging apps such as WhatsApp, Messenger, Viber, Telegram, Line and more, to provide a truly seamless and personalized customer experience.

My Role:

As a UX designer for Hoot's admin sign-up page, my job is to make it super easy and enjoyable for people to create an account. I focus on designing the page layout, buttons, and forms in a way that's simple to understand and navigate. My goal is to ensure that users can sign up quickly without any confusion, helping Hoot welcome new admins smoothly into their community.

The Features:

  • Unified Messaging : Connect with customers across multiple platforms using a single API and dashboard.
  • Centralized Management : Manage all messaging channels and conversations in one place with a single dashboard.
  • Personalized Engagement : Tailor communication and marketing campaigns based on customer data.
  • Seamless Interaction : Enable two-way communication for inbound and outbound conversations.
  • Actionable Insights : Analyze customer behavior, sentiment, and campaign effectiveness with advanced analytics.
  • Simplified Integration : Securely integrate with existing tech stack, CRM, and business tools for seamless operations and data synchronization.

Understanding the user

User Research

I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users. I’m designing for and their needs. A primary user group identified through research was working adults who don’t have time to go to café to stand in cue for ordering. This user group confirmed initial assumptions about JavaJunction customers, but research also revealed that time was one of the main factor which was limiting users from having coffee. Other user problems included interests in coffee or tea, or it becomes cool on the way to their office and challenge for them to ask money from there colleagues or friends with whom they have ordered.
